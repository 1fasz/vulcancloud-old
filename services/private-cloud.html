What Is It?

Your Own Cloud. Nobody Else's.

Public cloud platforms — AWS, Azure, Google Cloud — run thousands of customers on shared infrastructure. Your workloads compete for resources with anyone else on the same hardware. Your data sits in multi-tenant environments governed by policies you didn't write.

VulcanCloud's private cloud is single-tenant by design. Your compute, your storage, your network — dedicated entirely to your organization. Hosted in US-based data centers, managed by our team, governed by your compliance requirements.

It's the infrastructure model enterprise companies have used for years — now available to SMBs without the in-house team required to run it.

Key Benefits

  • ✦Single-tenant — no shared resources, no noisy neighbors
  • ✦US-based data centers — full data sovereignty
  • ✦Compliance-ready for HIPAA, SOC 2, and legal requirements
  • ✦Predictable performance — no resource contention
  • ✦Fully managed — no in-house infrastructure team needed
  • ✦99.99% uptime SLA backed by redundant architecture

Private vs. Public Cloud

Why Not Just Use AWS or Azure?

Public cloud is the right answer for some workloads. For compliance-sensitive, performance-critical, or data-sovereign requirements, private cloud wins on every dimension that matters.

🔒

True Data Isolation

Public cloud platforms share hypervisors, storage systems, and network fabric across thousands of customers. Private cloud means your data is physically and logically isolated from day one.

📋

Compliance Without Compromise

HIPAA, SOC 2, state bar requirements, and client data agreements often require documented evidence of data isolation. Private cloud makes that documentation straightforward.

⚡

Predictable Performance

No noisy neighbors. No burst pricing surprises. Your workloads get exactly the resources allocated to them — consistently, without competition from other tenants.

💰

Cost Predictability

Public cloud bills fluctuate based on usage, egress fees, and surprise charges. Private cloud from VulcanCloud is a flat per-month cost — the same bill every month, regardless of usage patterns.
