The Basics

What is Desktop-as-a-Service (DaaS)?

Desktop-as-a-Service (DaaS) delivers your entire work desktop — operating system, applications, files, and settings — from a secure cloud environment instead of a physical computer. You connect from any device and get a full, managed Windows desktop delivered over an encrypted connection. VulcanCloud uses Citrix technology to deliver this experience. Data never lives on the device you're connecting from.

How is this different from just using Microsoft Azure Virtual Desktop?

Azure Virtual Desktop (AVD) is an infrastructure platform — it provides the technology but not the management. Running AVD requires in-house or contracted expertise to configure, maintain, monitor, patch, and troubleshoot. VulcanCloud provides a fully managed service built on Citrix technology: we handle all infrastructure management, monitoring, patching, security, and support. You don't need to understand virtual desktop infrastructure to use VulcanCloud — that's the entire point.

Do I need to buy new computers?

No. Your existing computers — even aging laptops — can connect to VulcanCloud virtual desktops. Users just need a device capable of running a browser or the Citrix Workspace app, an internet connection, and their login credentials. Many clients transition to thin clients over time (which are cheaper and longer-lived than full PCs), but there's no requirement to purchase any new hardware before or during migration.

Pricing & Cost

How much does VulcanCloud DaaS cost?

VulcanCloud pricing is per-user per-month and includes the full managed desktop environment — compute, storage, Citrix licensing, security, monitoring, and support. Pricing varies based on the number of users, storage requirements, and applications hosted. Most clients in the 10–100 user range see per-seat costs between $60 and $150/month. Contact us for a tailored quote based on your specific environment.

Can I scale up or down?

Yes. Adding users takes hours, not weeks. Reducing user count can be done at the end of any billing cycle. This makes VulcanCloud particularly well-suited for firms with seasonal staffing (accounting firms during tax season), rapid growth, or frequent contractor usage. There are no hardware procurement delays — we provision capacity in the cloud.

Migration & Setup

How long does migration take?

Most migrations to VulcanCloud take 2–4 weeks from kickoff to go-live. This includes a discovery phase (assessing your current environment and applications), environment build, user acceptance testing, and the actual migration — typically performed after hours to minimize disruption. Larger or more complex environments may take longer. We've never missed a migration window for clients who complete the discovery phase on schedule.

Will my applications work in the virtual desktop?

Almost certainly yes. VulcanCloud's Citrix environment supports virtually any Windows application — including line-of-business software, legal practice management platforms (Clio, Time Matters), EHR systems (Epic, Athenahealth), tax software (UltraTax, Lacerte), portfolio management tools, and Microsoft 365. If an application runs on Windows, it runs in our environment. We test your specific applications during the discovery phase before any migration begins.

Security & Compliance

Is VulcanCloud HIPAA compliant?

Yes. VulcanCloud signs Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with healthcare clients and provides HIPAA-compliant infrastructure including data encryption at rest and in transit, audit logging, access controls, and documented security procedures. We provide written documentation of our security architecture for HIPAA compliance purposes. Our infrastructure is hosted in US-based data centers with SOC 2 certified facilities.

Can VulcanCloud help with cyber insurance requirements?

Yes — this is one of the most common reasons businesses come to VulcanCloud. Cyber insurance carriers increasingly require documentation of specific controls: MFA enforcement, data isolation, audit logging, endpoint security, and incident response procedures. VulcanCloud's managed DaaS environment enforces all of these by design, and we provide written documentation of security controls that clients use for cyber insurance applications, renewals, and audits.

Where is my data stored?

All VulcanCloud client data is stored in US-based data centers. We do not store client data in foreign jurisdictions. For clients with specific data residency requirements (government contractors, legal clients with international matters), we can discuss specific geographic hosting requirements. Data in transit and at rest is encrypted using industry-standard encryption.

Operations & Support

What's included in the managed service?

VulcanCloud's managed service includes: virtual desktop infrastructure provisioning and management, 24/7 monitoring and alerting, proactive patching and updates, security configuration and enforcement, user provisioning and deprovisioning, end-user technical support, backup and disaster recovery (depending on tier), and a dedicated support team with defined SLA response times. You don't need in-house IT expertise to operate the environment — that's our job.

What happens if something breaks?

VulcanCloud's operations team monitors your environment 24/7 and responds to incidents based on defined SLA tiers: 15-minute response for critical outages, 1-hour for high-severity issues, 4-hour for medium issues. Most infrastructure issues are identified and resolved by our team before users notice them. When end-user issues arise, your staff contacts VulcanCloud support directly — not your overloaded internal IT department.

What happens if the internet goes down?

Virtual desktops require an internet connection to connect — but so does any SaaS tool your team already uses. If your office internet fails, users can connect from any device with internet access: a mobile hotspot, a coffee shop, or their home network. VulcanCloud's infrastructure has redundant connectivity with a 99.99% uptime SLA on the cloud environment itself. The endpoint connection is the user's responsibility, the same as any cloud-based tool.
